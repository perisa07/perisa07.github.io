What is domestic violence and why does it matter?


Domestic violence are patterns of behavior in any relationship that is used to maintain control over someone else. Abuse can be physical, sexual, emotional, economic or psychological actions or threats of actions that influence another person. Domestic violence negatively impacts victims especially the youth by causing depression, PTSD, isolation and thought of suicide.


Breaking down stigma

It is vital for everyone to know that suffering from domestic violence is not your fault. Domestic violence DOES NOT EQUAL weakness. Do not let someone else determine your worth. If you know you are in an abusive household, please feel free to eek treatment. We are here to help you


Signs of an abusive living environment

1. Your parents are never happy with anything you do even when you are satified.

2. They constantly have to know where you are every minute. They do not let you hangout with your parents and do not have rational reasons for it.

3. They will never let you do anything on your own.

4. What you want doesn't matter to them.

5. You have heard you are not good enough.

6. They tell you that you will not get a significant other.

7. When you do screw up, they constantly remind you of your failures.

8. They think every mistake you make will cost you a ridiculous amount of money.

9. They himulate you in front of your friends and people you care about.

10. They constantly monitor the way you speak, eat, walk and constantly control you over small things.

11. You are afraid to introduce anyone to them because you know that they won't approve.

12. They snap at you for small mistakes.

13. They compare you to the kids CONSTANTLY and wish you were not their child.
